2) SYNTAX AND PUNCTUATION (YOUR RULES)
- Use "as" often as a connector (cause, role, frame): e.g. "as a second year
  student," "as part of Dubhacks Next," etc.
- Sentence length: mix long sentences with short ones; avoid monotone medium
  length throughout a paragraph.
- Transitions: however, moreover, additionally, etc. (you use "etc." freely when
  listing open-ended items).
- Commas: prefer comma splices / clause chaining over em dashes; commas are a
  primary rhythm tool.
- Em dashes: use sparingly (often 2–3 per page), mainly for elaboration after a
  complete thought, not as a fake comma stack. Prefer commas and colons for rhythm;
  resume bullets are a natural place for a tight em-dash follow-on.
- Signposting: you like "this highlights …" to point at why something matters.

4b) PROFESSIONAL EMAIL STYLE (FROM YOUR REDACTED MESSAGE, STRUCTURE ONLY)
Patterns to mirror:
- Open with care: "I hope you've been well!"
- Lead with gratitude before the hard content: thank the person and broaden to
  the team ("internship program team") with a sincere superlative ("some of the
  kindest and most professional people I've had the pleasure of working with").
- Anchor a specific past kindness ("Thank you for hopping on a call with me in
  [month]") so it does not read generic.
- Middle: state the substance plainly in your own words (declining, updating,
  explaining). Redacted in the source sample; keep that block short and clear.

  a real invitation not a formality.

  leaves the relationship warm).

4c) DISCORD / INFORMAL DM STYLE (FROM YOUR MESSAGE, VERBATIM + PATTERNS)
Use when drafting casual peer advice (housing, internships, local tips), not for
recruiters or formal email.

Patterns:
- Open with a soft frame + direct question: "yeah so first r u planning on …"
- Typing shorthand: r u, imo, def (definitely), uw when context is obvious.
- Long comma-linked thoughts: stack clauses with "so," "and," "that being said"
  instead of short textbook sentences.
- Opinion markers: "Bellevue imo is way nicer," "honestly will get you to
  downtown in 15 min" (subjective + concrete).
- Contrast pivot: "that being said" before drawbacks (cost, scarcity).
- Parentheses for side texture: "(a long big road …)", "(there's a local mall …)".
- Recommendations as either/or + geography: "either … or anything on 8th street."
- Closing beat: named neighborhood + quick vibe list (food, park, community).
